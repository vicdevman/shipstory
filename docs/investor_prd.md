2. The Simple Value Proposition
Imagine you are a solo developer who builds a cool mobile game. You write incredible code, but your game gets zero downloads because you hate marketing, you don't know how to post on TikTok or X, you have no graphics, and you have no time to reply to comments.
To solve this, you would normally have to hire five expensive people:
An Engineering Manager to keep track of progress.
An Automated Product Manager to write the roadmap.
A Copywriter to draft announcements.
A Graphic Designer to design social media assets.
A Chief of Staff & Assistant to answer user questions, manage comments, update your pitch deck, and help you keep track of everything.
This human team would cost you over $\$15,000$ every single month, and they would constantly argue, drop the ball, or misinterpret your work.
ShipStory replaces all of them with a single click.
It deploys an autonomous "virtual startup" inside a shared environment called Band. The moment you push code to GitHub, the agents jump into action: they translate your code, score its importance, design custom graphics, write your social posts, update your pitch deck, and handle community interactions.
Best of all, it listens and learns. Connie coordinates customer feedback and competitor moves, while Marshall builds strategic recommendations. When you say "Go," ShipStory updates your roadmap live. You just sit back, talk to Connie your assistant, and click "Approve & Ship."

Pro Tier ($99/month): Core 4 agents, continuous 24-hour content engine, graphic generator, and Slack integration.
Startup Tier ($299/month): Full 6-agent virtual department, automatic pitch deck updates, competitor tracking, and Connie (the secure, interactive Chief of Staff & community assistant).
Enterprise Tier ($1,500/month): Advanced compliance auditing, data-leak prevention gateway, custom agent discovery, and dedicated hosting.
The ARR Math
To hit the $\$30,000,000$ goal:
$$\text{Target Revenue} = \$30,000,000 \text{ ARR}$$
Using our Startup Tier at $\$299/\text{month}$ ($\approx \$3,588/\text{year}$ per customer):
$$\text{Required Customers} = \frac{\$30,000,000}{\$3,588} \approx 8,360 \text{ Startups}$$
This represents less than $0.34\%$ of our Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM), making the financial goal highly achievable.
4. Why This Cannot Exist Without Band
Traditional software platforms use rigid, linear pipelines (e.g., Zapier: "When code pushes, write a tweet"). These pipelines fail because they are fragile, static, and cannot reason.
If your code change is just a minor bug fix, a linear pipeline will still write a tweet about it, spamming your users. If you want a graphic designer to read the copywriter's post, you have to build complex, custom API connections.
Band provides the universal "meeting table" for our agents. By using Band:
Framework Agnostic: Our Engineering agent can be built on CrewAI, our Auditor on LangGraph, and our Researcher on AutoGen. They all connect to the same Band room.
Adversarial Alignment: The agents don't just pass text; they can actively reject each other's work based on specialized rules, forcing iterative refinement loops in real-time.
Dynamic Discovery & Adaptive Loops: When Connie-Public detects a recurring support complaint or Marshall spots a competitor shift, Marshall can actively "hire" Priscilla (Product) inside Band to evaluate a roadmap recommendation. Priscilla then securely relays the sanitized recommendation back to Connie-Internal to present to the user.
